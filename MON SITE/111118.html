Conception

The project is the idea of four men, the intention was to try to integrate what the young people of our age had to go through during the First World War, what it was to fight against an enemy you did not even know .

Our today society is build on our previous acts and we must keep epxloring its most violent part of it. With the eye of the hero, the spectator is guided trough the trenches, confronted to the same hope and despair as thousands of men should felt during those years. I started to work on this feature film after the final clap has been made. One of the director came to me with a will of an end credit that should keep the dirt and the loneliness of the hero’s emotions but also bring the power of hope depsite the situation. We made some brainstorming to find an idea that was meaningfull to the movie and esthetically pleasing.

Realisation

The intentions was to keep the idea of mud, dirt and globally darkness of the movie but to add a touch a hope and light. After a few meetings we decided to exploit the raw material of the set, I’m speaking here of mud and smoke, gunpowder and rain.

After some tests I proposed a first version of a dark smoke moving around the screen and the credits, changing color as a cloudy sky but getting more and more dirty. I had a particular gloomy vision of the end of the film. But as we spoke about the ending it began the appear that the edn was really to focus the spectator on the hopefull side of the story. With this complete feedback, I started working deeper on the project and scheduled real work day.

So firstly I designed some differents smoke in AfterEffect, some heavy, some light, some quick and clear and some slow and dull. Then I edited an entire 5 minutes long sequence of only smoke to give me a base of work for the actual credits.

Lesson n°1

Focus on possibilites Not problems.

Final product


Once my base and the credits (with their right timecode) assembled, the fun part came: playing with the smoke. I recover the first smokes I made and begin to add layers and layers to play with the title, adding depth to my composition. Then I moved onto the colors, all to give this impression of elevation during the whole sequence, we come from the grave to go to a clear sky. I used mostly greens, yellows and browns with some touch of blue.

The final product is an end credit of 4 minutes with a smoke effect and differents alpha layers to acheive the depth of it. The movie is expected for the end of 2019.
